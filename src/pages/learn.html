What is Computer Science?

Computer Science isn’t really about “hacking the mainframe.” It’s the study of computation and information—covering algorithms, data, hardware, software, and systems.

A Short History of Computer Science

  • 1800s: Ada Lovelace writes the first algorithm for Babbage’s “Analytical Engine.”
  • 1930s–40s: Alan Turing lays the foundations of computing; WWII sees the first programmable machines.
  • 1950s–70s: Mainframes and programming languages (like FORTRAN, C) power business and science.
  • 1980s–90s: PCs and the internet bring computing to homes and schools worldwide.
  • 2000s–Today: Cloud, AI, mobile, and quantum computing reshape how we live, work, and connect.

From punch cards to AI, CS has grown from theory to powering almost every part of modern life.
