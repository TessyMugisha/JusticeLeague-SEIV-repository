FAQ & Contact

Commonly asked questions and ways to get in touch.

How do I stand out?

Projects, internships, open-source contributions, and clear communication. Show real work and explain your decisions.

How do I find internships?

Use campus career services, referrals, and apply early with tailored resumes. Track applications and follow up.

What if I’m switching into CS?

Start with fundamentals (Python + data structures), take an outside course, and build 1–2 small projects to show momentum.

Do I need a powerful laptop?

8–16GB RAM is fine to start. Schools often provide lab machines and remote servers for heavy tasks.

Myths vs. Reality

“CS is only for math geniuses.”

You’ll use math (logic, discrete, sometimes stats), but persistence and practice matter more—skills grow with time.

“It’s just coding all day.”

CS includes design, teamwork, documentation, testing, and understanding users. Communication and product sense are huge.

“You have to pick one path forever.”

Skills transfer across web, mobile, data, DevOps, security, and product. Many people move as interests evolve.
