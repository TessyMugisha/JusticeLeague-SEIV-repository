Student Life

What It’s Like Being a CS Major

From morning lectures to evening study groups—here’s a realistic snapshot of the rhythm, courses, and skills you’ll build.

A Day in the Life

  • Morning: Lecture/Lab (Data Structures, Systems, Math)
  • Afternoon: Team project using Git & VS Code
  • Evening: Study group, office hours, coding practice

Core Courses & Skills

  • Programming (Python/Java/C++)
  • Data Structures & Algorithms
  • Software Engineering (team projects)
  • Operating Systems & Networks
  • Databases & SQL

Tip

Join a coding club or hackathon early—great for projects and finding study buddies.

FAQ

Do I need a powerful laptop?

8–16 GB RAM is fine to start. Most schools provide lab machines and remote servers for heavier tasks.

How much math will I take?

Usually Calculus I–II and Discrete Math at minimum; some programs add Linear Algebra and Probability.
