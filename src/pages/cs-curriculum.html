Typical CS Curriculum

Schools vary, but most programs move from fundamentals to systems and specialized electives. Here’s a simple, realistic roadmap.

Year 1

Foundations

  • Intro to CS & Programming I/II (Python/Java)
  • Discrete Math (logic, sets, proofs)
  • Calculus I–II + core GE

Goal: think like a programmer; write clean basics.

Year 2

Core Building Blocks

  • Data Structures (trees, hash maps, complexity)
  • Computer Architecture (CPU, memory)
  • Probability/Statistics

Goal: efficient code + how computers work.

Year 3

Systems & Scale

  • Algorithms (graphs, greedy vs. DP)
  • Operating Systems (processes, concurrency)
  • Databases (SQL, transactions)
  • Networks (TCP/IP, HTTP)

Goal: reason about larger systems end-to-end.

Year 4

Depth & Projects

  • Security or Software Eng I/II
  • Capstone/Senior Project
  • Electives to specialize

Goal: specialize and ship portfolio work.

Common Electives

AI & Data

  • Intro to AI / Machine Learning
  • Data Mining / Visualization
  • Natural Language Processing

Systems & Cloud

  • Distributed Systems / Cloud Computing
  • Parallel Computing
  • DevOps & SRE

Security & Privacy

  • Cryptography
  • Network/App Security
  • Digital Forensics

Human & Product

  • Human–Computer Interaction (HCI)
  • UX Engineering
  • Product Management for Engineers

Notes

Advising

  • Meet your advisor at least once per semester.
  • Map courses, prereqs, and key deadlines early.

Staying on Track

  • Check your degree audit each term (credits, GPA, core).
  • Verify you’re pacing to graduate on time.

Specialization

  • Curious about switching or adding a focus?
  • Ask about prereqs & course availability; try an elective first.
